Breaking Bad

Breaking Bad is an American television series created and produced by Vince Gilligan. It originally aired on the AMC network from January 20, 2008, to September 29, 2013. The series is a crime drama that revolves around the transformation of high school chemistry teacher Walter White into a ruthless drug lord.

The show has received critical acclaim and has won numerous awards, including 16 Primetime Emmy Awards.

Reviews

Breaking Bad has received critical acclaim and has won numerous awards, including 16 Primetime Emmy Awards.

Rotten Tomatoes gives the series a 98% approval rating based on 87 reviews, with an average rating of 9.4/10. The site's critical consensus reads, "Breaking Bad is a gripping portrayal of a man driven to the edge by his family's financial struggles and his own moral decline."
